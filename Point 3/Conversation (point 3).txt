Prompt:
A farmer has 3 chickens, 2 pigs, and 1 cow. 
 Each chicken has 2 legs, each pig has 4 legs, 
 and each cow has 4 legs. How many legs are there in total?
 
 Solve this problem step by step:
 
 1. First, determine the number of legs for each type of animal.
 2. Each chicken has 2 legs. How many legs do 3 chickens have?
 3. Each pig has 4 legs. How many legs do 2 pigs have?
 4. Each cow has 4 legs. How many legs does 1 cow have?
 5. Now, add up the legs from all the chickens, pigs, and the cow.
 What is the total number of legs?

Response:
Here's the step-by-step solution:

1. **Legs per animal:**
   - Chickens: 2 legs each
   - Pigs: 4 legs each
   - Cows: 4 legs each

2. **Chicken legs:** 3 chickens * 2 legs/chicken = 6 legs

3. **Pig legs:** 2 pigs * 4 legs/pig = 8 legs

4. **Cow legs:** 1 cow * 4 legs/cow = 4 legs

5. **Total legs:** 6 legs (chickens) + 8 legs (pigs) + 4 legs (cow) = **18 legs**    

**Therefore, there are a total of 18 legs on the farm.**

Response:
## Solving the Chicken and Cow Problem

**Problem:** A farmer has 100 animals, consisting of chickens and cows. There are 300 legs in total. How many chickens and how many cows does the farmer have?

**Subproblems:**

1. **Define variables:**
    * Let 'c' represent the number of chickens.
    * Let 'w' represent the number of cows.

2. **Set up equations based on the given information:**
    * **Equation 1 (Total animals):** c + w = 100
    * **Equation 2 (Total legs):** 2c + 4w = 300 (Chickens have 2 legs, cows have 4)

**Solution Paths:**

**Path 1: Substitution**

* **Step 1:** Solve Equation 1 for 'c': c = 100 - w
* **Step 2:** Substitute this value of 'c' into Equation 2: 2(100 - w) + 4w = 300    
* **Step 3:** Simplify and solve for 'w': 200 - 2w + 4w = 300  =>  2w = 100  =>  w = 50
* **Step 4:** Substitute the value of 'w' back into Equation 1 to find 'c': c + 50 = 100  =>  c = 50

**Path 2: Elimination**

* **Step 1:** Multiply Equation 1 by -2: -2c - 2w = -200
* **Step 2:** Add this modified equation to Equation 2: 2w = 100  =>  w = 50
* **Step 3:** Substitute the value of 'w' back into Equation 1 to find 'c': c + 50 = 100  =>  c = 50

**Path 3: Guess and Check**

* **Step 1:** Start with an initial guess for the number of cows.
* **Step 2:** Calculate the number of chickens based on the total number of animals. 
* **Step 3:** Calculate the total number of legs based on the number of chickens and cows.
* **Step 4:** Adjust the guess for the number of cows based on the difference between the calculated total legs and the actual total legs.
* **Step 5:** Repeat steps 2-4 until the calculated total legs match the actual total legs.

**Evaluation of Paths:**

* **Path 1 (Substitution):**  Clear and efficient, systematically eliminates one variable to solve for the other.
* **Path 2 (Elimination):**  Similar to substitution but focuses on eliminating a variable directly by adding equations.
* **Path 3 (Guess and Check):**  Time-consuming and requires multiple iterations, especially if the initial guess is far off.

**Conclusion:**

Both Path 1 (Substitution) and Path 2 (Elimination) are the most promising solutions. They are both straightforward, systematic, and provide the correct answer quickly.  Path 3 (Guess and Check) is not recommended due to its inefficiency.

**Answer:** The farmer has 50 chickens and 50 cows.
